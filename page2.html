Top 10 Places To Visit!

Mysore Palace

Timings : 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM It is an incredibly breathtaking example of Indo - Saracenic style of architecture. It was built in the year 1912 for the 24th Ruler of the Wodeyar Dynasty and is counted amongst one of the biggest palaces in the country. It is the second most visited historical monument visited by both local and foreign tourists after the Taj Mahal. It is renowned for its light & sound show and vibrant Dussehra celebrations.

GRS Fantasy Park

Timing: 10.30 am - 6 pm It is a theme based amusement park and offers really amazing rides and activites making it a perfect weekend getaway.

Karanji Lake

Timing: 8:30 am – 5:30 pm (Tuesdays Closed) This lake was built as a percolation tank by the Mysore King. Since 1976, it has been maintained by the Mysore Zoo authority. It is spread across an area of 90 hectares of lush greenery and rich fauna; out of 90 hectares of area, 55 hectares is the water spread area while the foreshore area is around 35 hectares.Just a leisure walk around the lake also promises to give you a few peaceful moments amidst the beautiful natural setting.

Kishkindha Moolika Bonsai Garden

Timing: 9 am - 12 pm and 4 pm to 6 pm It is a favorite spot for many nature lovers in the city and makes for a very good education trip to take with your kids. It was established in 1986. The miniature plan reflects the cosmic creation, the play of celestial paradoxes near, yet far, more, yet less, known, yet unknown, Small yet Big”. Today it is spread over 4 acres.

Shivanasamudra Falls

Timing: Open 24 hours This beauty is made of two waterfalls named Gaganachukki and Bharachukki. It is known for being India's first hydroelectric power station. If lucky enough one can witness the playfulness of monkeys as well so keep your belongings safe.

Brindavan Garden

Garden timings: 7:00 AM - 8:00 PM (Weekdays) 7:00 AM - 9:00 PM (Weekends) Spread over 60 acres. Built across the notable river of India, Cauvery, it took around five years to complete the project.Water fountains adorn the finely manicured grounds of this sprawling area and some of the main flora found across the Gardens includes beautiful Ficus trees and flowering plants like celosia, marigolds and bougainvilleas. Musical Fountain Show: 6:30 PM - 8:00 PM (Weekdays) 6:30 PM - 9:00 PM (Weekends)

Chamundi Hills

Timing: 7:30am-2:00pm, 3:30-6:00pm 7:30-9:00pm They are named after the Goddess Chamundeshwari. It covers a wide angle of the Mysore City. Take your camera and binoculars with you to admire the flora to its fullest. The hilltop provides a gorgeous view of the Karanji Lake and can be reached by road or by taking the stairs (which is for adventourous people).

Lalitha Mahal

Timing: 8:30 AM to 6:00 PM This is the second largest palace in Mysore near Chamundi Hills and has been in existence since 1921. It was designed to imitate St. Paul’s Cathedral in London.It is pure white in colour and it was transformed into a heritage hotel in 1974 and is now maintained by the Government. It's balcony offers a spectacular view of Chamundi Hills to the left and Mysore City to the front.

Melody World Wax Museum

Timing: 9:30 am to 7:30 pm Dedicated to music, Melody World Wax Museum is one of the most delightful Mysore Tourist attraction. It hosts a display of a wide array of musical instruments, life size statues of musicians playing various instruments in out of the ordinary sets make this museum a place worth visiting. This museum has been since 2010 but the building is said to be more than 90 years old.

Tour of the Silk Factory

Timing: 10am–6:30pm Silk and Mysore go together like caterpillars in a cocoon. Mysore is world known for Mysore Silk so one must take a tour of this factory to learn and familiarize themselves with the knowledge of making silk products.
